The Cardio

One's choice in cardio is typically flexible as you can do any exercise(s) of your choice that can sustain your heart rate above a certain threshold for a certain period of time. Cardio can be divided into two categories:

  • High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
  • Steady-State Cardio

HIIT

HIIT is a type of cardio that is performed explosively. With this type of cardio, one should aim to keep their heart rate above 80% of their max heart rate. It can be performed with any type of exercise where one can sustain such a heart rate for short bursts of time. These workouts are generally shorter than steady-state cardio workouts. For optimal results, total workout time can range from 10 to 30 minutes including warm-up and cooldown depending on the intensity at which the workout is performed. Examples include sprints, burpees, box jumps, bodyweight squats, skipping, etc.

Steady-State Cardio

Steady-state cardio is generally low to moderate intensity cardio that is performed over a longer period of time. With steady-state, one should aim to maintain a heart rate between 60% to 80% of their max heart rate. For optimal results, total workout can range from 30 to 45 minutes. Examples include jogging, swimming, cycling, rowing, elliptical machine, stepping machine, etc.
